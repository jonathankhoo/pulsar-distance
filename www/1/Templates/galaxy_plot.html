As no spacecraft from Earth has ever travelled out of our Galaxy it is impossible for us to take a photo of our Galaxy from the outside. However, astronomers can see many other galaxies in the Universe and have evidence to show that our Galaxy is a spiral galaxy. Even though we cannot photograph our own Galaxy, we can make a diagram of its structure. Such a diagram is given below which represents looking down on our Galaxy from above. The yellow circle shows the position of our Solar System. The blue circle indicates the position of PSR J0437-4715 using your dispersion measure determination. Note how close PSR J0437-4715 is to our Earth when you consider distances on the scale of our Galaxy! Some of the other pulsars that you will study are much further away.
