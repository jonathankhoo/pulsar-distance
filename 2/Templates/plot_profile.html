The pulses from PSR [@PULSAR_NAME] have been separated into eight different observing frequencies. In order to calculate the distance to [@PULSAR_NAME], you need to determine how much earlier the highest-frequency pulse arrives compared with the lower-frequency pulses. Type the arrival times and corresponding frequencies into the "Enter frequency and time" boxes. When all the arrival times have been entered, click "Plot" to plot the values.

To make more accurate measurements than in the training session you can select to plot only one observing frequency using the pull-down menu under "Select Frequency". Click here if you wish to re-read the instructions on the previous page. If this pulsar is affected by interference then you can click here to choose a different observation.
